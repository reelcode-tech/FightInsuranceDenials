# Condition Community Expansion

## Why This Matters

Insurance denial pain is not evenly distributed. Some of the best observatory signal will come from disease communities where:

- treatment is expensive
- care is long-term
- equipment or home support is essential
- insurers often challenge medical necessity, duration, or setting of care

## High-Value Condition Areas

### Neurodegenerative and Caregiving

- ALS
- Alzheimer's disease
- dementia caregiving communities
- Parkinson's disease communities
- MS communities

These areas matter because they often surface:

- DME denials
- home health and caregiver support gaps
- long-term care coverage problems
- infusion and specialty medication barriers
- Medicare Advantage and retiree-plan friction

## Public Sources To Prioritize

- The ALS Association
- Alzheimer's Association
- AARP Medicare and caregiving forums
- targeted Reddit caregiver and diagnosis communities
- investigative reporting about high-cost chronic and progressive disease care

## Extraction Priorities

When collecting observations from these areas, pay extra attention to:

- durable medical equipment
- respiratory support
- long-term care
- home care and home health
- cognitive care support
- specialty drug access
- Medicare Advantage restrictions
- caregiver financial burden

## Weighting Guidance

- Official nonprofit guidance should be treated as benchmark and support evidence.
- Public patient or caregiver narratives should be treated as medium-weight narrative evidence.
- Complaint-site records should be treated as lower-weight clustering input only.
