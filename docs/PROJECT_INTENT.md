# Project Intent

Last updated: 2026-04-20

## Why this exists

Health insurance denials are designed to isolate patients.

People get a denial, panic, and then are expected to fight a large insurer with almost no precedent, no comparable stories, and no usable data. This project exists to change that.

FightInsuranceDenials is meant to be:
- a public-interest record of real denial experiences
- a patient tool for finding comparable cases fast
- an appeal-support layer that turns scattered stories into usable evidence
- a foundation for sellable analytics that can pressure insurers through hospitals, lawyers, employers, and regulators

## Primary user

The primary user is a stressed patient or caregiver who just got denied.

That means the product must optimize for:
- speed to understanding
- emotional clarity
- strong trust signals
- concrete next steps

## Product principles

- Proof over prose
- Patient clarity over internal analytics jargon
- Real data over invented examples
- One strong action per section
- Public value first, monetization second

## What success looks like

The product is successful when:
- a patient can understand the value in under 10 seconds
- a patient can find a relevant matching denial pattern in under 2 minutes
- a patient can copy usable language or a citation into an appeal packet
- the public site feels calm, credible, and human-designed
- the data-products layer contains at least one export/dashboard/report that looks truly valuable to an organization
